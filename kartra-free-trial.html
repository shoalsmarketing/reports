Understanding the Pricing Philosophy

All-in-one platform pricing differs fundamentally from point solution economics. Individual tools might cost less in isolation, but integration complexity and data fragmentation create hidden costs that consolidated platforms eliminate. Evaluating investment requires comparing against the true total cost of fragmented alternatives rather than individual tool prices.

The pricing structure reflects infrastructure costs, development investment, and support requirements. Cloud hosting for page builders, email delivery infrastructure, video hosting bandwidth, and continuous feature development all contribute to operational expenses that subscription fees must cover. Understanding this context helps evaluate whether pricing represents fair value exchange.

Contact-based pricing tiers scale with business growth. Starting plans accommodate new businesses building initial audiences, while higher tiers serve established operations with substantial customer bases. This scaling approach prevents overpayment during early stages while ensuring capacity exists when growth occurs.

Common Questions About Pricing

Are there hidden fees beyond subscription costs?

Subscription fees cover platform access comprehensively. Payment processing fees apply to transactions (standard credit card processing rates through connected processors like Stripe or PayPal). No additional platform fees apply to sales, memberships, or subscriptions you process.

What happens if I exceed contact limits?

Approaching limits trigger notifications enabling proactive management. Temporary overages typically receive grace periods for cleanup or upgrade decisions. Persistent overages require plan upgrades to maintain full functionality. Contact pruning strategies can optimize existing tier utilization.

Can I downgrade plans if needed?

Plan changes apply at billing cycle boundaries. Downgrades take effect at next renewal, though contact limits on lower tiers must accommodate existing list sizes. Data remains intact during transitions; only capacity constraints change.

Do annual plans offer refunds if I need to cancel?

Refund policies vary; reviewing current terms before annual commitment is advisable. Some partial refund provisions may exist for early termination. Monthly billing provides maximum flexibility for uncertain commitment situations.

How do agency features work for client management?

Growth tier and above enable sub-account creation for client work. Each client receives isolated workspace with dedicated resources. Billing consolidates under agency account while clients access only their environments. Markup opportunities exist for agency service offerings.

Are there discounts available?

Annual billing provides consistent discounts versus monthly rates. Promotional periods occasionally offer additional savings. No permanent discount programs exist publicly, though direct inquiry may reveal situational opportunities.
